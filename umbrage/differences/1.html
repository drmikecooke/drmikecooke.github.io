Difference operators

Backward

The relation between \(D=d/dx\) and \(\nabla=\mathbb1-T^{-1}\), where \([Tp](x)=p(x+1)\), is given by Taylor series that can be compacted into: \[\exp(-D)=T^{-1}=\mathbb1-\nabla\]

We also have \([\exp(xD)p](n)=p(n+x)=[(\mathbb1-\nabla)^{-x}p](n)\). We can formally binomial expand: \[p(n+x)=[(1-\nabla)^{-x}p](n)=\sum\limits_{k=0}^\infty \binom{-x}k (-1)^k [\nabla^kp](n)\]

From the operator expansion: \[S=\sum[Sq_k](0)Q^k\] applied to \(T^x\): \[T^x=\sum[T^xq_k](0)\nabla^k=\sum q_k(x)\nabla^k\]

we read off the basic polynomials for \(\nabla\): \[q_k(x)=\binom{-x}k (-1)^k\]

This gives \(q_0(x)=1\).

A more specific formula is: \[q_k(x)=\frac1{k!}\prod\limits_{n=0}^{k-1}(x+n)\]

This makes it fairly easy to confirm \(\nabla q_k=q_{k-1}.\)
