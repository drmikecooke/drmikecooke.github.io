Difference operators

Intro

Difference operators are usually defined in terms of translation operators. We use a unit step difference, often denoted \(h=1\). Other step sizes can be accomodated through scaling \(x\).

There are three main types of difference operators, where \(\mathbb1=T^0\), the identity operator:

Backward:
\(\nabla=\mathbb1-T^{-1}\)
Central:
\(\delta=T^{1/2}-T^{-1/2}\)
Forward:
\(\Delta=T^1-\mathbb1\)

For interpolation, and other numerical analysis problems such as solving differential equations, we actually want to find \(T^a\) in terms of these difference operators.
