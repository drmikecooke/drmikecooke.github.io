Binet 2

In: \[I(z)=\int\limits_0^\infty\left[\frac 1{1+t^2}\frac{(-\ln(1-\exp(-2\pi zt))}{\pi }dt\right]\] I notice that \(1/(1+t^2)\) has an indefinite integral: \[\int\limits_0^s\frac 1{1+t^2}dt=\arctan(s)\] This can be shown by the subsitution \(t=\tan(\theta)\). An integration by parts gives: \[I(z)=\int\limits_0^\infty\left[\frac{\arctan(t)}\pi\frac{2\pi z\exp(-2\pi zt))}{1-\exp(-2\pi zt) }dt\right]\] We absorb \(z\) into \(t\), and rearrange the exponentials: \[I(z)=\int\limits_0^\infty\frac{2\arctan(t/z)}{\exp(2\pi t)-1 }dt\] This is the second Binet form for \(I\).
