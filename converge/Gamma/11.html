Gamma function

Binet 1 Stirling series

\[\ln\Gamma(z)=(z-\tfrac12)\ln(z)-z+\tfrac12\ln(2\pi)+I(z)\] with \[I(z)=\int\limits_0^\infty\left[\frac1 2-\frac1t+\frac{1}{\exp(t)-1}\right]\frac{\exp(-zt)}{t}dt\]

We also define the Bernoulli series:

\[\frac t{\exp(t)-1}=\sum\limits_{k=0}^\infty\frac{B_n}{n!}t^n\] What we are calling \(B_n\) are sometimes denoted \(B_n^-\), which is the value of the Bernoulli polynomials evaluated at zero. \(B_n^+\) are the numbers from the polynomials at one. It happens that the only difference is that \(B_1^\pm=\pm1/2\). Apart from \(B_1\), we also have \(B_{2m+1}=0\).

Clearly \(B_0=1\) so: \[I(z)=\int\limits_0^\infty\left[\frac{t}{\exp(t)-1}-B_0-B_1t\right]\frac{\exp(-zt)}{t^2}dt \\ =\int\limits_0^\infty\left[\sum\limits_{m=1}^\infty\frac{B_{2m}}{(2m)!}t^{2m}\right]\frac{\exp(-zt)}{t^2}dt\] Unfortunately the Bernoulli series only converges for \(t\lt2\pi\), so the further derivation of the Stirling series from: \[\int\limits_0^\infty t^{s-1}\exp(-zt)dt=\int\limits_0^\infty \left[\frac tz\right]^{s-1}\exp(-t)\frac{dt}t=\frac{\Gamma(s)}{z^s}\] is somewhat moot.

Doing the replacement, the Stirling series is: \[I(z)\approx\sum\limits_{m=1}^\infty\frac{B_{2m}}{(2m)!}\frac{\Gamma(2m-1)}{z^{2m-1}}=\sum\limits_{m=1}^\infty\frac{B_{2m}}{2m(2m-1)}\frac{1}{z^{2m-1}}\] using the connection between \(\Gamma\) and factorial. The wavy equals sign indicates that the series converges nowhwere. Truncated versions become very good approximations as \(z\rightarrow\infty\) with the remainder being given by an integral similar to \(I(z)\), but with further terms of the Bernoulli series subtracted off.
